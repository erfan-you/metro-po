Breaking News:

Notorious Cybercriminals on the Loose It has come to light that two are actively involved in a wide range of online criminal activities Although concrete evidence of their physical crimes is not yet available, there are suspicions of their involvement in this domain as well.

Saeed Biabani

Mohammad Mehdi Zolghadr

Criminals

Name: Saeed Biabani

Birthdate: August 16, 1995

Name: Mohammad Mehdi Zolghadr

Birthdate: July 23, 2002

Activities

Their most prominent illicit activity involves collaborating with several European drug cartels in advertising and brokering the sale of narcotics to both regular people and wholesale distributors. Additionally, Mohammad Mehdi Zolghadr is accused of selling the production formula for famous Iranian drugs to European drug mafias

Mohammad Mehdi Zolghadr

Activities

He is also suspected of extorting money from famous individuals and even European politicians by removing their Instagram accounts. On the other hand, Saeed Biabani is implicated in cyber-attacks against Iranian government websites and participating in DDoS attacks. He has also collaborated with his longtime friend, Parham Moradzadeh Sarabi, in phishing schemes, laundering the proceeds through Café Bazaar and the crypto market in a highly intricate manner that took Iranian authorities years to uncover.

Activities

Furthermore, Saeed Biabani achieved significant success in advertising for European drug cartels and earned a substantial income through these endeavors. These two individuals have also been involved in significant transactions on the dark web, the nature of which is still unclear.

News

Furthermore, confirmed evidence suggests that Saeed Biabani illegally traveled from Germany to the UK and then returned to Europe shortly afterward, although the purpose of this trip remains undisclosed.

News

Additionally, there are documents indicating that these individuals promoted counterfeit euro banknotes and received a percentage of the sales.

Our teams

Several other crimes have been attributed to these two individuals, although they are yet to be confirmed. Investigations in this regard are ongoing, and relevant authorities are working tirelessly to gather more evidence to convict these cybercriminals.
